I like to build new technologies that not only enable new paths for engineers or scientists, but also that have a postive impact on people's life.

I am currently an Audio Software Engineer at Tesla. There, I develop audio software tools, signal processing and machine learning pipelines, and tests to ensure bit perfect audio quality in vehicles. I also develop and enhance sound user interfaces to help guide users' actions; which include, chimes and speech recognition systems.

Before working at Tesla, I interned at Oscillo Biosciences, where I had the honor of working side-by-side with Prof. Edward Large and Dr. Ji Chul Kim. There, I worked on developing dynamical systems oscillators that synchronize to music or human rhythmic interactions. I delivered an iOS app that integrates the dynamical model to re-train rhythmic synchronization on patients with aphasia.

I have a B.S in Computer Science and a B.S in Applied Mathematics from UC Davis. I am currently pursuing a M.S in Artificial Intelligence at USC. My main focus is to carry out research in machine listening: sound detection, localization and audio-visual learning. Feel free to learn more about my research.
